Physical units in astrophysics

Physical units in astrophysics are used to measure and describe astronomical phenomena and objects.

Some of the most common units include light years, solar masses, parsecs, Kelvin degrees, and the speed of light. Astronomy and astrophysics involve measurements of extremely large and small quantities, so it is necessary to use unit systems that are useful for both macroscopic and microscopic measurements.

The proper physical units are crucial in astrophysics to understand the scale and features of the objects being studied, as these are often immense and difficult to imagine without an appropriate frame of reference.
